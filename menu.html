Caffè Mocha

Enjoy the richness of the classic mocha, and finish with a drizzle of caramel syrup for extra sweetness and warmth.

Cappuccino

When a barista steams the milk for a cappuccino, microfoam is created by introducing very tiny bubbles of air into the milk, giving the milk a velvety texture. The traditional cappuccino consists of a single espresso, on which the barista pours the hot foamed milk, resulting in a 2 cm (3⁄4 in) thick milk foam on top.

Caffè Latte

And like most classics, part of its appeal comes from its simplicity. A caffè latte is simply a shot or two of bold, tasty espresso with fresh, sweet steamed milk over it. Some prefer to add syrup or extra espresso to the recipe. Some maintain that it is entirely perfect as is.

Espresso

Its rich flavour, lingering aroma and caramelly sweetness make it the perfect foundation for lattes, cappuccinos and all our espresso-based beverages. But you can also enjoy it all by itself – indeed, that might be the best way to discover its nuances
